The Process

Simple. Fast. Unrestricted.

From donated hardware to a functioning CS classroom — our process is designed to move quickly and get out of the way.

01

Computers donated or sourced

We collect end-of-lease refurbished machines from tech companies, accept public donations, and partner with certified refurbishers. Minimum spec: capable of running Linux, Python, and VS Code.

02

Schools apply & get matched

Title I schools and under-resourced districts submit a simple program description. We prioritize documented CS programs and student need. No bureaucracy — just a clear partner agreement.

03

Devices delivered, ownership transferred

Computers are granted — not loaned. Schools take permanent ownership with no content filtering or MDM restrictions imposed by us. One annual impact update is all we ask in return.

Our Mission

Bridge the digital divide in K-12 education

Schools too often receive locked-down devices that cripple real computer science learning. Students can't install a compiler, run a local server, or explore an OS. OpenKeys exists to fix that.

"To bridge the digital divide in K-12 education by providing unrestricted computers to schools through partnerships that empower students with real-world computing access."

We operate through fiscal sponsorship, meaning donors receive tax-deductible status while we maintain full independence to manage school partnerships and hardware directly.

No MDM. No filters. Ever.

Unrestricted access is a core principle — not a feature. Schools agree by policy, not because we lock devices down from afar.

Fast from application to delivery

Using fiscal sponsorship, OpenKeys can be operational within 60–90 days — no IRS filing required to get started.

Schools take full ownership

We grant devices permanently. Schools own them, maintain them, and use them however best serves their CS program.

Light reporting, real accountability

One annual page — student count, courses taught, basic outcomes. That's all we ask. No burden, meaningful data.
